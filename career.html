Travis Scott: Career as a Rapper

Travis Scott’s career as a rapper skyrocketed after he signed with Kanye West’s GOOD Music as a producer, before eventually signing to Epic Records in 2012. His early work with Kanye helped him gain recognition in the hip-hop world, but it was his own unique sound and style that made him a standout artist in the genre.

In 2013, Travis released his first mixtape, *Owl Pharaoh*, which was met with critical acclaim. His second mixtape, *Days Before Rodeo*, further solidified his status in the hip-hop community, featuring tracks like "Mamacita" and "Drugs You Should Try It". But it was his debut studio album, *Rodeo*, released in 2015, that truly cemented his position as one of the top rappers in the world.

Rodeo featured hit singles like "Antidote" and collaborations with artists such as Kanye West, Justin Bieber, and Young Thug. His music blends elements of hip-hop, trap, and psychedelic sounds, which became a signature style. Over the years, his albums such as *Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight* (2016), *Astroworld* (2018), and *Utopia* (TBA) have consistently broken records, dominated charts, and earned him multiple Grammy nominations.

Travis Scott is not just a rapper, he’s a cultural phenomenon. His contributions to the genre go beyond music; he’s an innovative producer, fashion icon, and a staple of modern pop culture. His live shows are famous for their high-energy performances and the unique visual experiences they provide, with his *Astroworld* tour being one of the most successful tours in recent years.

Travis Scott's career trajectory continues to soar, with collaborations with major brands like Nike and McDonald’s, alongside his musical ventures. He has reshaped the landscape of rap and music, making his mark as one of the most innovative and influential artists of his generation.
